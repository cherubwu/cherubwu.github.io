# ✨ *Scottish Fantazy*

## 🌫️ **Day 7 --- Nessie Whispers & Highland Tempests**

> *"Storms outside, steady hearts within."*

Morning broke in gold and mist --- a spectacular sunrise during our forest-side breakfast. Before the train, we explored the other side of the village: wide, wild golf fields and a riverside path where cows looked up as we waved hello.\
Then came a message that froze my heart --- our ferry to Orkney might be cancelled because of an approaching storm. The sky dimmed a little, but hope stayed afloat.

Our train arrived eight minutes late, but the view made up for everything: glens and lochs rolling past, the very tracks where the Hogwarts Express once steamed through the clouds.\
**Inverness** greeted us with wind and promise. We dropped our bags at our new home, the Ness View Apartment --- after a comical battle with the lock! Our host popped by to check on the brand-new bathtub (fate smiling again: a bath tonight!).

Then --- Ness River, here we come!\
We ran along the banks under clouds thick with mystery, heading for the legendary **Loch Ness.** The fog turned the water silver, and somewhere in that shifting mist, Nessie stirred --- or maybe it was just our reflections grinning back.

We jumped off at **Urquhart Castle**, its ruins perched over the loch like a secret from the 6th century. There, I discovered a new favourite potion: whiskey hot chocolate 🥵. Between the warmth, the wind, and the spell of the scenery, I moved in a dream --- sometimes too slow, sometimes too fast, heart racing with magic and caffeine.

Then disaster: after a quick toilet break, we lost each other! No signal, no clue. Panic rose --- until a voicemail from Charlie broke through. Found. Saved. But oh --- a storm of emotions followed.

The sun returned like an apology. We made peace on the climb to **Inverness Castle**, the city glowing below like a Highland jewel. For a moment, everything was still.\
Then the real storm came. By the time we reached our “top-rated” restaurant, the wind was roaring, the rain unrelenting. Charlie was exhausted and grumpy; dinner was a flop. But we held hands through it all, laughing between gusts, trying not to blow away on the walk back to our Ness-side nest.

That night, the wind howled like an orchestra outside. We curled up, watched *The Addams Family,* and let the storm play its gothic symphony.\
A spooky, cozy night --- perfectly us. 💋🌧️
