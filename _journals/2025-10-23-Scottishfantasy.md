# ✨ *Scottish Fantazy*  
*A whimsical travel journal of rain, love, and laughter across the isles.*

---

## 🌞 **Day 1 — Cambridge & the Beef Bourguignon Redemption**

> *“A day that began with nerves, and ended in laughter and stew.”*  

I woke up at **5 a.m. without an alarm**, heart fluttering like a fiddle tune.  
Charlie was arriving today — somewhere between *sky and planet* — and I was a swirl of nerves, hope, and caffeine.  

The **sunrise from Cambridge to Heathrow** was a watercolor of honey and lavender, as if Scotland herself whispered,  
> *“Aye, make it a good one.”*  

When we finally met, the world clicked into focus.  
Brunch at the **top-rated Half Cup café near King’s Cross** felt like a dream — **Oreo pancakes**, **fried-chicken waffles**, and **smoothies** shared between laughs.  

Later, the *London sun*, in rare generosity, smiled down upon us.  
By evening, Cambridge called us home, where our culinary ambition simmered in a pot: *Beef Bourguignon à la Chaos*.  

It was perfect — until we realised we were **locked out**.  
Standing outside, holding a pot of French glory, we looked at each other like tragic poets in a domestic comedy. 
Love is… waiting for me to fetch the spare key while your dinner loses its steam. 🥹🥂

## 🌧️ **Day 2 — Cerulean Rain & Cyberpunk Greenwich**

> *“We chased the sunrise and found rainbows in the drizzle.”*  

We woke **early to chase the sunrise**, jogging through still-sleepy **Cambridge** streets as the sky blazed **pink, orange, and fire-red**.  
He was buzzing with energy; I was already questioning all my life choices and realizing I desperately needed to restart my exercise routine.

The sky burned brighter as we ran — a painter gone mad with color — and by the time we stumbled back, I was ready for a long shower. We left a little late, smelling of shampoo and victory.  

Then, *London time.*  
We met **Charlie’s best friend and our classmate** in the City — a trio armed with coffee and a questionable sense of direction. From Liverpool Street to the Gherkin, along the river Thames past Big Ben, the London Eye, Tate Modern, and St Paul’s — we walked the city’s heartbeat.  
We wandered **Covent Garden** and **Soho**, neon signs winking like mischievous spirits.  
Lunch at **Japes Pizza** became an endless laughter reel: too much cheese, too many stories, exactly enough happiness.  
 
Then the sky began its art project again. The Queen’s walk  shimmered; **the Thames turned cerulean**, reflective and mysterious — like a melancholy AI poet from a future London. We took the Uber ferry down the Thames to Greenwich, the fog rolling in to swallow towers, ships, and maybe a few timelines.

> We called it *“The Cyberpunk Hour of Rain.”*  
> Somewhere between raindrops and reality, we lost track of time — maybe that *was* the Greenwich magic. ⏰💙

## 🌦️ **Day 3 — The Wrong Train to the Right Day**

> *“Sometimes the wrong train takes you to the right sunset.”*  

A **massive breakfast**, a confident smile… and the *wrong train.* Classic us.  
But the journey turned cinematic: the **sea widened** as we crossed from England to Scotland, **Newcastle’s gothic spires** waving us onward through mist and legend.  

And then — *woooha!* — **Edinburgh arrived** faster than our hearts could. 

We dropped our bags at a downtown apartment — No.1 Apartments George IV Bridge , right above the famous Elephant House — storyland downstairs, our little nest upstairs.
Before dinner, we climbed to **Calton Hill** where the drizzle turned gold. We took tons of photos — wind in our hair, city at our feet, the kind of pictures you keep for rainy mornings and anniversaries.

First adventure as a domestic duo: grocery shopping together — treasure-hunting at the 美麒麟 Asian market, then I snagged fresh veg from Sainsbury’s near the university. He headed back to cook (chef mode: on), and I followed with clinking bags and giggles in the cobbled dusk.
Back home, our **homemade hotpot** bubbled like a love potion — spicy, steamy, gloriously chaotic. 

 Outside, Edinburgh whispered; inside, everything felt warm and right. 

> “One Day,” we said — but it already felt like forever. 🫶🌇🔥

## 🏞️ **Day 4 — From Arthur’s Seat to the Sea (and the Toilet Tragedy)**

> *“Sunshine, squirrels, and an inconvenient plot twist.”*  

A morning jog, a **classic brunch**, and then the noble climb of **Arthur’s Seat**.  
The wind kissed us; the sunshine applauded. “Hi, Sunshine!” we waved like fools.  

We strolled through **galleries**, **Edinburgh Castle**, and the **National Museum**, where I bowed respectfully to **Dolly the sheep** — a national icon in wool.  

In **Princes Street Park**, we met a curious squirrel… and the tragedy of a *closed toilet.*  
My dignity suffered. My throat too.  

But evening turned the story around.  
We wandered hand-in-hand through the lamplight, our **romantic night walk** humming with laughter and gentle quiet.  

At **Portobello Beach**, the sky deepened into **blue hour** — not a five-star beach, maybe three, but perfectly ours.  
Then: **hot bath. steam. soft laughter.**  
The day ended like a kiss under the moon. 🫧💙🌊  

---

## 🌬️ **Day 5 — Dundee Dramas & Perth Fantasies**

> *“Every misstep a new melody.”*  

We **missed the train to Dundee** — destiny’s way of keeping things interesting.  
**Costa coffee** saved us, and soon we were playing **poker on ScotRail**, cards and laughter between raindrops.  

**Dundee** greeted us with rain, art, and wind sharp enough to etch memories.  
We biked the city and seaside, chasing pockets of blue sky and salt.  

By the time we reached **Perth**, the world exhaled.  
Our **townhouse hotel** was a dream — garden whispers, tartan grace, and a breakfast lounge soaked in morning gold.  

We crossed the **bridge over the River Tay** into **Kinnoull Hill Woodland Park**.  
Charlie, my beloved trickster, swore, *“It’s not high.”* Lies.  
We followed a **schoolgirl-turned-forest-spirit**, her laughter echoing through moss and mist, and somehow — up the wrong path — we found the right view.  
**Perth spread below like watercolor.**  

Dinner at **Cardo** arrived early, as did we.  
**Soup, rosé, steak**, and finally — *the divine ending:* **chocolate marquise with pear and lemon sorbet.**  

> A night of flavor, warmth, and stolen glances.  
> Maybe the stars above Perth saw it too — our little fantasy, sealed with our first night’s kiss. 💋✨
